Tesla Reports Strong Q4 Earnings, Stock Jumps 12%

Published: January 25, 2024

Tesla Inc. (TSLA) reported better-than-expected fourth-quarter earnings yesterday, sending shares soaring 12% in after-hours trading. The electric vehicle giant posted earnings per share of $0.71, beating analyst estimates of $0.64.

The company delivered 484,507 vehicles in Q4, a 20% increase from the previous quarter. CEO Elon Musk highlighted the success of the Model Y and announced plans for expanded production at the Austin Gigafactory.

"We're seeing unprecedented demand for electric vehicles globally," Musk said during the earnings call. "Our investment in battery technology and autonomous driving capabilities positions us well for 2024."

The strong performance also benefited other EV stocks, with Rivian (RIVN) and Lucid Motors (LCID) rising 5% and 7% respectively in extended trading. Traditional automakers like Ford (F) and General Motors (GM) also saw modest gains as investors remain optimistic about the EV sector.

Tesla's energy storage division reported record installations, generating $1.4 billion in revenue. The company's solar panel business also showed improvement with 35% growth year-over-year.

Looking ahead, Tesla plans to begin production of the Cybertruck at scale and expects to maintain vehicle delivery growth of at least 20% annually. The company also announced a $25 billion investment in charging infrastructure across North America.
